Shea Fruits: Current Facts

Botanically known as Vitellaria Paradoxa, shea tree, also known as shea nut tree or shea butter can live for up to 300 years. They take 20 to 30 years to reach maturity and begin producing fruits. Once they begin, they can produce for the next 200 years. The fruit contain a relatively large and oily nut, used to make Shea Butter. For a Sub-Saharan African country like Ghana, the fruit provides an important source of income for the people.

Nutritional Value & Application

Shea fruit is harvested primarily for the oil within its kernel, an extraction process that can take up to 8 hours and involves multiple steps. The fruits are rich in carbohydrates, protein and fat as well as potassium and antioxidant phenols.
